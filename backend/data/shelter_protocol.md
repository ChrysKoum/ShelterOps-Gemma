# Shelter Protocol

Intake staff should record disaster type, zone, household size, languages, immediate needs, and private notes. Household size is used for sleeping mats, blankets, meals, water, and hygiene kits.

Assign sleeping areas by zone and keep households together when possible. Vulnerable persons such as infants, elderly residents, pregnant people, people with disabilities, and people with chronic illness should receive human review during the same shift.

Shift handoffs should include priority, open tasks, supply needs, language support, safety notices, and the team responsible for follow-up. Avoid exposing private notes outside internal systems.

Documentation support can be logged for residents who lost ID or records, but public requests must not mention resident documents.
